# T2 sample notes — durable growth investing

**Q: What comes first when evaluating a position meant to be held for
years — the chart or the business?**

A: The business. A multi-year thesis has to survive on durability of
competitive position, quality of the balance sheet, and the reliability of
free cash flow generation; a bullish MACD cross without a fundamental
quality case is not sufficient grounds to underwrite a multi-year hold.

**Q: How much should tax considerations weigh on a long-horizon call?**

A: At most ten percent, and only as downside protection — for example,
preferring to trim a long-held winner across two tax years rather than
realizing the full gain at once — never as the primary driver of the
investment decision itself.

**Q: What invalidates a long-term thesis?**

A: A deterioration in the underlying business quality — margin compression
that doesn't reverse, loss of competitive moat, or a balance sheet that
can no longer fund the growth story — not a short-term drawdown in the
stock price, which is expected and irrelevant to the thesis.
