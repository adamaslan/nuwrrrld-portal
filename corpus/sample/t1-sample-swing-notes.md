# T1 sample notes — swing trading

**Q: When should a swing trader treat an RSI reading as an oversold bounce
setup rather than a falling knife?**

A: An RSI reading below 30 is only actionable as an oversold bounce when it
coincides with ADX above 25 confirming an established downtrend rather than
a range — a low RSI inside a low-ADX range is noise, not a signal. Wait for
the first higher low on declining volume before sizing an entry.

**Q: How should position size change when volatility is elevated?**

A: When realized volatility sits in the top third of its trailing range,
cut standard position size by half and widen the stop proportionally
rather than tightening it — a tight stop in a high-volatility regime gets
run before the setup has time to work.

**Q: Does tax treatment factor into a 1-60 day swing decision?**

A: No. Tax weight is zero on trades held under two months; the entire
decision is technical merit — entry, stop, and target relative to the
measured move — with tax treated as irrelevant noise at this horizon.
